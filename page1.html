The Expert App Design
PROBLEM STATEMENT

"Historically, Users have difficulty finding experts in diverse fields in order to get advice on various subject matters. Offen responses are delayed & the advice is unreliable on online forums & the experience is not interactive.  

POTENTIAL SOLUTION
An application which enables both experienced and inexperienced users to post a question, chat quickly, or schedule a meeting (audio/video call), with verified experts of various fields to get advice on a particular task that user wants to accomplish.
Competative Analysis
I started out with competitor analysis to understand the product, the current market, who the competitors are, what strategies they use, what value they provide, understanding their - and mine - strengths and weaknesses, and what are the risk & opportunity, where my app would fit in. I gathered this data by analyzing there key objectives, overall strategy, market advantage, marketing strategy, SWOT profile.
The two main competitors I have identified for the scope of this project are
Just Answer & 6YA app.

User Surveys & Interviews


User Surveys:
User surveys give us quantitative data informing us about “what is happening”. I conducted survey prior to interviews to get a feel for the users’ goals and pain points which provides a useful basis for scripting interview questions that can dig deeper into survey findings. This method can provide near-instant feedback and help with either redefining or confirming current design thoughts.

User Interviews:
I conducted user interviews which provide me a qualitative data by closely observing the users attitudes, body language which informs about “why it is happening”. 

I created a set of questions and I used Survey Monkey to do the survey.
After gathering data from survey and user interview I did research analysis. In order to organize and analyze my user research data, I created a series of affinity maps

RESEARCH ANALYSIS

AFFINITY MAPPING

User Persona, Task Analysis, User Flow
To create a successful web app it is important to understand the types of people who will use the app. To do this I created User Personas which help establish empathy with users & help prioritize functionality based on users needs and goals. For the scope of this project, I created 2 different personas with a focus on the major needs of the most important user groups.
 Card Sorting & Site Map 

Card sorting method helped to evaluate or generate ideas for the information architecture of a product or service. The aim is to find patterns that give clues about the most logical and user-friendly way to organize my application. which helped to build the structure, decide what to put on the homepage, and label categories for navigation. And also helped me to determine how users look at the product or service and investigate their mental models so I can present the information/content to them in an optimal way. This ensures a good flow of information and a smooth navigation experience. 

TOOLS USED- Optimal Workshop for card sorting                                                                     

Design Ideas & low-Mid Fiedlity Prototype
Iteration phase
After Research & Discovery phase I have collected enough data to find the solution of the problems what features/functionality will work & what not, how I can prioritize & organize the information. Next in iteration phase I sketched some design ideas. After reviewing these ideas I selected a design to sketch and develop wireframes and user flow in more details and added annotations. Sketching helps to work rapidly and evalute more ideas.

TOOLS USED- Paper pencil for sketching design ideas                                                                                             Balsamiq for wireframing & prototyping                                                                                                 
Mid fidelity Prototype
Interactive Prototype For Usability Testing

 After mid-fidelity prototype, I developed interactive prototype to conduct interactive tests with inperson and remote test users. The prototyte did not emphasize on UI & Design principles. The focus was to test the user task flow to identify the usability of the app.

The usability tests were recorded with user permission for later analysis. The information was analyzed using Affinity map. This helped me to group information isolated from recordings, allowing me to trace out patterns of behavior or sentiments across multiple participants.  

The prototypes were tested constantly and iterated progressively. Working prototype was created using Adobe XD tool for the test. 

During the tests, usability issues were identified and iterations were made to solve these issues and re-test for validation.

Final Design

After the iteration phase & fixing all the challenges I encountered, it is time to think about UI design. I was unsure which design icons, color palette to use so I conducted preference test which helped me to make the final decision. I also relieased the need of an onboarding process to enable new users to start using the app efficiently right from the start.

RETROSPECTIVE

What went well?
• User interview went well which gave me a lot of information & helped me in brainstorming different ideas based on user needs & goals. Feedback from usability testing confirmed that my app is easy to navigate with aesthetic design.
What did not go well?
• Figuring out the usability and basic functionality was more challenging as I was unsure which design flow to follow, whether I should go with the idea where user can directly call to an expert without booking an appointment which will save there time, but the downside of this flow is user will not get the chance to choose the expert based on their criteria, the call will be directed to any available expert in that field. And doesn’t give any information about expert's expertise and experience.
• Or with the flow where user select categories, choose an expert based on expert's experience, rating, price etc. book an appointment & wait for experts' confirmation this is long processes & take some time to accomplish the task. The positive side of this flow is it gives reliability & users satisfaction which is a very important factor for the success of the app.
• At first, I did detailed work on both side (advice seekers & expert or adviser) of the app as the feature & function for both sides will be different. I was in a dilemma whether I should design for experts' point of view or seekers point of view. Finally, during the prototyping phase, I must choose one side to finish the project on time, so finally I chose the advice seekers side.
• It took a lot of time to identify pros & cons of each design & put them on test to decide which one is more suitable for the app. It was challenging to create a prototype with adobe XD efficiently (on free version).
What can be improved?
• Making decision at early stages without spending too much time on extra & unnecessary part which would have finished the project earlier.
