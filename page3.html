The Voclab App Design

PROBLEM STATEMENT

The average active vocabulary of an adult needs  around 20,000 to 40,000 words, building vocabulary is long and tedious processes many folks give up as the whole processes is not fun and engaging. There is a need to build vocabulary for people below average vocabulary as it is one of the major factors to progress in career.



PROBLEM SOLUTION

“I believe creating an application that allows users to customize and personalize for learning new words and phases through definition, context and usage, at there own pace in fun and easy way” 

Competative Analysis
I started out with competitor analysis to understand the product, the current market, who the competitors are, what strategies they use, what value they provide, understanding their - and mine - strengths and weaknesses, and what are the risk & opportunity, where my app would fit in. I gathered this data by analyzing there key objectives, overall strategy, market advantage, marketing strategy, SWOT profile.
The 5 main competitors I have identified for the scope of this project are

 1. vocabulary builder
2. Galvanize
3. visual vocabulary
4. Duolingo (language learning app)
5. magoosh (GRE preparation app)
 
User Surveys & Interviews

User Interviews:
We learn through repitation & revision, differnt people have different methods of learning some are visual learner other has auditory learning style. I interviewed users to find out what is there learning style? which style they prefer & why? The answers to this questions helped me to incorporate & prioritize the features & function of the app. I got the mix answers they used all the methods based on there surroundings or scennario. for example while riding bike they use audio mode or while waiting for bus they would like to play a quick game.

After gathering data from user interview I did research analysis.

TOOLS USED -  Post Notes


User Persona, Task Analysis, User Flow
To create a successful web app it is important to understand the types of people who will use the app. To do this I created User Personas which help establish empathy with users & help prioritize functionality based on users needs and goals. For the scope of this project, I created persona with a focus on the major needs of the most important user groups.
 Card Sorting & Site Map 

Card sorting method helped to evaluate or generate ideas for the information architecture of a product or service. The aim is to find patterns that give clues about the most logical and user-friendly way to organize my application. which helped to build the structure, decide what to put on the homepage, and label categories for navigation. And also helped me to determine how users look at the product or service and investigate their mental models so I can present the information/content to them in an optimal way. This ensures a good flow of information and a smooth navigation experience.

Design Ideas & low-Mid Fiedlity Prototype
Iteration phase
After Research & Discovery phase I have collected enough data to find the solution of the problems what features/functionality will work & what not, how I can prioritize & organize the information. Next in iteration phase I sketched some design ideas. After reviewing these ideas I selected a design to sketch and develop wireframes and user flow in more details and added annotations. Interactive Prototype For Usability Testing

 After mid-fidelity prototype, I developed interactive prototype to conduct interactive tests with inperson and remote test users. The prototyte did not emphasize on UI & Design principles. The focus was to test the user task flow to identify the usability of the app.

During usability testing I took notes on issues related with the app & observed users behavior

The prototypes were tested constantly and iterated progressively. Working prototype was created using Adobe XD tool for the test.  

During the tests, usability issues were identified and iterations were made to solve these issues and re-test for validation.

After the iteration phase & fixing all the challenges I encountered, it is time to think about UI design. Summery

The whole idea of the app is that making a list of words you want to master. You can create the lists from words suggested by the app based on your quiz report. Or from news/blogs/stories or you can also search any word & add it into the list for later practice.
the words can be practice with 3 different modes 
1) by reading news & blogs 
2) by playing challenges
3) by listening in audio mode
the user will learn & practice by answering the multiple choice question & images.
they can also play word challenges & compete with other participants & earn reward points.
The app will generate a progress report based on the word you mastered & the words you need practice.
